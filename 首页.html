A selection of cuisines

Fried liver

Fried liver is quite a famous Beijing snack, indeed the name is quite strange, said that "fried" is actually a bowl of thick soup, today Xiaobian will talk about the fried liver and several famous fried liver shops in Beijing, so that you can understand the history of fried liver, you can also have a good meal

Shabu lamb

Shabu lamb can be juxtaposed with the famous Peking duck, which is the most inseparable delicacy in the heart. Copper pot charcoal fire, hand-cut fresh mutton, dipped in special sesame sauce ingredients, eating sweaty, teeth and cheeks fragrant, this is probably what Lao She wrote: good shabu mutton, "can eat imagination, eat poetry, shabu slice dipped into the mouth of sesame sauce, is the essence of heaven and earth combined by animals and plants."

Sugar burns

The sugar is fragrant, sweet, soft and non-sticky, suitable for the elderly. According to legend, as far back as the Chongzhen period of the Ming Dynasty, the Hui named Liu Dashun came from Nanjing with grain ships along the North-South Grand Canal to the ancient town of Tongzhou, which is Tongzhou directly east of today's Beijing.

Sheep scorpion

Sheep and scorpion hot pot can definitely be called one of the special foods of old Beijing. The sheep scorpion hot pot in old Beijing is a must: "Its meat is tender but not greasy, its bones are myelin but not smooth, and its soup is rich and fragrant." "Just thinking about it makes people drool

Gold medal soy sauce chicken

Not a single chicken can leave Guangdong! The Peninsula's famous soy sauce chicken, with crispy skin and juicy, is fragrant and makes foodies drool! What's more, it smells like chicken!

Roast duck

Roast duck is a special dish in Beijing and Nanjing, belongs to Beijing cuisine or Jinling cuisine, the dish is known as "the delicacy of the world" and is famous at home and abroad, its color is slightly yellow, soft and fragrant, and it is rolled with other vegetarian foods to eat, which is a common dish for banquets, and it is a home-cooked delicacy

Recommended food in Beijing

Bean juice

Beijing bean juice is a unique food in Beijing, which is made by grinding mung beans to make vermicelli or dough flour, after removing the starch, leaving a light green and cyan soup, which is fermented and boiled. It is said that as early as the Qianlong period, bean juice has been introduced to the royal family

Donkey rolling

Donkey rolling is made of glutinous rice flour, and the skin is thin and covered with various fillings that are relatively sweet, and it is a cooked food

Old Beijing court cheese

Cheese was originally pioneered by ethnic minorities in the north, and is a semi-coagulated food made from cow and sheep milk, which is creamy and creamy and nutritious, and melts in the mouth. Cheese was used in the courts of the Yuan, Ming and Qing dynasties and was once a treasure of royal dining

Beijing's food hotspot

Pea yellow

Pea yellow, also known as pea huanger, is a traditional snack in Beijing and a seasonal product in Beijing's spring. Peas are usually grated, peeled, washed, boiled, sugar-fried, curdled and diced. After the finished product, the appearance is light yellow, delicate, melts in the mouth, sweet taste, cool taste.

Exploding belly

Pop belly is a famous traditional snack among Tianjin and Beijing style snacks. Exploding belly explosions were recorded as early as the Qianlong era of the Qing Dynasty. Beijing Tianqiao has "Exploding Belly Stone", door frame alley has "Pop Belly Yang", and "Pop Belly Feng" and "Pop Belly Man" are the most famous

Kidney bean roll

Kidney bean rolls are traditional Chinese medicinal snacks made with kidney beans and bean paste. The color is snow-white, the texture is soft and delicate, and the filling is sweet and refreshing, which was once the imperial point of Cixi
